Away fans are housed in the South Stand Lower at Old Trafford, accessed via Warwick Road. The view is decent and the stand is covered. Legroom is tight by modern standards — Old Trafford is showing its age — but the atmosphere from the away end can be decent when numbers are up.

The allocation varies depending on the fixture and your club's away membership scheme. Check your club's official ticketing pages well in advance — tickets sell out fast.

02
Getting There
Train, tram and car
1
Arrive at Manchester Piccadilly or Manchester Victoria — both served from across the country via Avanti, LNER, and Northern Rail.
2
From Piccadilly, take a local train to Manchester Oxford Road or Deansgate — both around a 20-minute walk to the ground.
3
Follow Chester Road south from Deansgate towards Old Trafford. The ground becomes visible well before you arrive.
4
Away fans should approach via Warwick Road. Stewards will direct you to the South Stand turnstiles.
Book Trains via Trainline
1
Take the Metrolink tram to Old Trafford stop on the Altrincham or Airport lines — right next to the ground.
2
Services run from Piccadilly Gardens, St Peter's Square, and Deansgate-Castlefield in the city centre.
3
Trams get very busy on matchdays. Allow extra time and consider arriving earlier than usual.
1
Old Trafford is well signposted from the M60 motorway — exit at Junction 7 (Stretford/Old Trafford).
2
Parking near the ground is extremely limited. Book in advance via JustPark or similar.
3
Consider parking near a Metrolink stop — Salford Quays area works well — and taking the tram the last leg.

04
Practical Tips
Before you go
Book tickets early. Away allocations at Old Trafford go fast, particularly for clubs with large followings. Check your club's official ticketing pages as soon as fixtures are announced.
Arrive early. Security queues at the South Stand can be slow on big matchdays. Give yourself at least 45 minutes before kick-off.
Card only inside. Concessions inside the ground are card-only. Make sure you have contactless or mobile payment ready.
Getting home. Trams and trains fill up very quickly after the final whistle. Check your last available service before you go and build in extra time.
Away colours. Fine inside the ground. Exercise common sense in the surrounding streets — particularly on Warwick Road immediately post-match.
Away End Verdict
3.5/5
Good Day Out. Creaking Ground.

Old Trafford is a legendary name but the ground itself is tired. The South Stand Lower is functional rather than impressive — you'll be fine, but don't expect the modern facilities of a newer stadium. The pub situation is more limited than you'd expect for a ground this size, so plan ahead. The atmosphere is what you make it — bring your voice.
