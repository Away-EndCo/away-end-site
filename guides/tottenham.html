The Away End

Location
South Stand Upper
Away Allocation
Around 3,000

Away fans are housed in the South Stand Upper — one of the best away ends in the Premier League. The Tottenham Hotspur Stadium is a genuinely world-class venue and the views from the away section are excellent throughout. High up but with an unobstructed view of the entire pitch.

The stadium itself is a significant upgrade on the old White Hart Lane and the away end benefits from that. Facilities are modern and well-maintained. If you can get a ticket, the stadium experience alone is worth it.

Getting There

1
Take the Victoria line to Seven Sisters then the Overground one stop to White Hart Lane station — right outside the ground.
2
Alternatively, Seven Sisters station is around a 15-minute walk to the ground via the High Road.
3
From central London, allow 45-60 minutes depending on your starting point and tube connections.
4
Away fans enter via the South Stand — well signposted from White Hart Lane station.
Book Trains via Trainline
1
Driving to Tottenham is not recommended. North London traffic on matchdays is severe and parking near the ground is extremely limited.
2
If you must drive, park further south (e.g. near a tube station) and travel the last section by tube.
3
The M25 / A10 / A1010 are the main approach routes but expect significant delays.

Practical Tips

The tube is your best friend. Victoria line to Seven Sisters, Overground to White Hart Lane. Simple, fast, reliable.
Drink in central London and head up on the tube — far easier than navigating options near the ground.
Allow extra time. Tube services are frequent but platforms at Seven Sisters fill up fast on matchdays.
The stadium is genuinely impressive. The South Stand Upper gives you a great view of one of the best new grounds in England.
Post-match. The Overground and tube are quick — services are frequent and the journey back into London is straightforward.

Away End Verdict

4/5
Stunning Stadium. Tricky Pubs.

The Tottenham Hotspur Stadium is one of the best venues in the Premier League — modern, well-designed, and with an excellent away section. The pub situation nearby requires some planning but the tube connection makes the whole day very manageable. If your club gets an away allocation here, it's one of the better away day experiences you'll have in London.
