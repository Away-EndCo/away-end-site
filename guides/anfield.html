View
Good — elevated upper tier

Away fans are housed in the Anfield Road Stand, which was significantly expanded and reopened during the 2023/24 season. The new upper tier gives away supporters a proper elevated view of the pitch — this is one of the better away ends in the Premier League now that the rebuild is complete.

The Kop is directly opposite, which means you'll have a clear sightline to it in full voice. That alone makes the trip worthwhile. Entry is via the Anfield Road turnstiles — check your ticket for your exact gate.

02
Getting There
Train, bus and car
1
Arrive at Liverpool Lime Street — the main intercity station, served by Avanti, LNER, and TransPennine from across the country.
2
Anfield is around 3 miles from Lime Street — too far to walk. Take a bus or taxi from the city centre.
3
Alternatively, Merseyrail to Kirkdale (Northern Line from Lime Street Low Level) gets you within a 15-minute walk of the ground.
4
Check current timetables before travelling — services vary on matchdays and last trains back fill quickly after the final whistle.
Book Trains via Trainline
1
The 26 and 217 bus routes run from Liverpool city centre to Anfield on matchdays — check Merseytravel for current stop locations and times.
2
Buses get very busy in the hour before kick-off. Leave plenty of time or consider a taxi from the city centre instead.
1
Anfield is accessible from the M57 and M62 motorways. Follow signs for Anfield from the ring road.
2
Parking near the ground is very limited. Community schemes fill quickly — book in advance via JustPark if possible.
3
Better option: park in the city centre and take a bus or taxi out. Avoids the post-match traffic chaos around Anfield entirely.
03
Pubs for Away Fans
Verified recommendations
The Arkles
Near Ground
Anfield Road — roughly 5 minutes from the ground. One of the few pubs in the immediate vicinity that has historically admitted away fans. Verify access on the day — policies can change on matchdays. Arrive early, it fills fast.
BrewDog Liverpool
City Centre
A reliable away-friendly option in the city centre. No issues for visiting fans, decent selection. Works well as a base before heading out to Anfield.
The Fly in the Loaf
City Centre
Wetherspoon on Hardman Street. Plenty of space, no issues for away fans, cheap. A solid option for a pre-match drink before making the trip to the ground.
⚠️
Options near Anfield are limited for away fans. The general advice is to drink in the city centre and head to the ground closer to kick-off. Always verify access before turning up in away colours.
04
Practical Tips
Before you go
Anfield is cashless throughout. Food and drink inside the ground is card or contactless only — make sure you're set up before going in.
Check the bag policy. Liverpool FC operates strict bag restrictions. Check the official away fan guide on their website before you travel — it's updated each season.
Allow time at the turnstiles. Away end queues build quickly in the 30 minutes before kick-off. Aim to be at the ground at least 45 minutes early.
Drink in the city centre. Options near the ground are limited and access for away fans can be inconsistent. Settle your pre-match drinks in town and travel out.
Plan your return. Trains and buses fill fast after the final whistle. Check last available services before you travel and build in extra time.
Away End Verdict
4/5
One of the Great Away Days.

Anfield is one of those grounds every away fan should experience at least once. The atmosphere is genuine, the expanded Anfield Road Stand now gives travelling supporters a proper platform, and watching the Kop in full voice from directly opposite is something you won't forget. The main frustration is logistics — getting there takes planning and pub options near the ground are thin. Sort your drinks in the city centre, plan your transport, and Anfield will look after the rest.
